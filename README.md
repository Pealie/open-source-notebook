# Open source, automation, and social resilience

This repository contains an essay arguing for broader adoption of open source approaches, with a focus on the near future rollout of business and industrial automation and the risk of economic disruption.

## Summary

The essay makes the case that open source is not only a software development model but a governance and market design choice. Under conditions of rapid automation, open source can help societies reduce dependence on single vendors, strengthen supply chain security, widen access to productive tooling, and increase the pace of diffusion for safe, auditable methods.

It also takes seriously the counter arguments: open source does not automatically imply security, sustainability, or accountability. The essay therefore proposes concrete routes to make open source adoption socially robust, including procurement reform, funded maintenance, and standards aligned assurance.
